# devops testing

start by establishing the context:
* gather info & ideas from the company: run a test strategy retrospective
* identify people involved in delivering the product
  * include devs, business analysts, designers, product owners, operation engineers, scrum masters, delivery managers, etc
* devops affect all roles, try to find out what devops means to them?
* a manager will focus on the outcome, a developer will focus on the implementation
* => managers should provide vision & rationale for change, but avoid dictating the specifics
* those involved in impltementing hte vision should focus on the next incremental improvement
* find out which driver for devops is most important for them: knowing motivations help prioritising new dev practices
* find out who is open for changes, & where & why change wouldn't be welcome

## test strategy retrospective

* learn more about the specific goals of DevOps for your organisation
* check that everyone is on the same page by:
  * determining whether everyone in the team knows what the test strategy is
  * agreeing on which aspects of the strategy have been implemented
* even if there's no documented test strategy, people have ideas about testing: check people periodically

how to do it:

* gather the team
* explain you're going to visualise the test strategy
* on a board, draw a line IDEA --> PROD (timeline that reflects SDLC)
* use coloured sticky notes:
  * color A: in our test strategy & we're doing it
  * color B: in our test strategy & we're not doing it
  * color C: not in our test strategy, but should be
* devs put sticky notes onto the timeline, at the point they think the test activity should occur

after that, ask questions:
* what terminology? same problems but different terminology? why?
* why are activities not implemented?
* do we want to include other strategies?
* are activities missing from the visualisation?
