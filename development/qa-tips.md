# qa tips

* don't be passive: make your concerns know, and know when to fold
* help executives & managers figure out what they need, figure out how to meet that need
* guide testing to best serve the product's needs
* if you have diverse demographics for your users, have diverse demographics for your testers too
  * study actual users / targeted demographics
  * invite a handful of representative customers for a simple usability test
* don't be passive: losing vigilance is abdicating responsibility
* vigilance in testing: being a good skeptic; reject certainty

## development

* users don't complain about failed unit tests
* so focus on integration, network fault tolerance, the device, ux and user expectations

## product mgmt - marketing

some testers should be familiar with the product, so they can become real experts with it

some testers shouldn't be familiar with the product:
* so UX can be tested from the POV of the average user
* so (to the contrary) the product can be scrutinized under every aspect (expert breaking)

product expertise and testing expertise are 2 different but complementary ingredients

## have data

* data proves what you say, without data you're just arguing
* you need a complete definition of "done" for each feature
